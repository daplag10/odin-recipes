Sinigang na Baboy

Description

Sinigang na Baboy is a classic Filipino sour soup known for its refreshing tamarind-based broth.

This comforting dish combines tender pork with vegetables like kangkong, radish, and eggplant, creating a perfect balance of sour and savory flavors.

It's best enjoyed hot with steamed rice, especially on rainy days.

Submitted by Juan Dela Cruz

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly, cut into chunks
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish (labanos), sliced
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 2 long green chili peppers
  • 1 pack sinigang mix (tamarind soup base)
  • Fish sauce to taste

Directions

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add pork and simmer for 30–40 minutes until tender.
  2. Add onions and tomatoes. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in the sinigang mix and fish sauce.
  4. Add radish and eggplant. Cook for 5–7 minutes until vegetables are tender.
  5. Add kangkong and chili peppers. Cook for 2–3 minutes.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice.
